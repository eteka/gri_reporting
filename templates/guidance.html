GRI Reporting Principles

Principles for Defining Report Content
Stakeholder Inclusiveness

Identify stakeholders and explain how you've responded to their reasonable expectations and interests.

Sustainability Context

Present performance in the wider context of sustainability challenges.

Materiality

Cover topics that reflect significant economic, environmental, and social impacts.

Completeness

Include all information necessary for stakeholders to assess performance.

Principles for Defining Report Quality
Accuracy

Information must be sufficiently accurate and detailed for assessment.

Balance

Reflect positive and negative aspects of performance fairly.

Clarity

Make information understandable and accessible to stakeholders.

Comparability

Enable comparison over time and with other organizations.

Timeliness

Report on a regular schedule so information is available when needed.

Verifiability

Gather and record information so it can be examined for quality.

Understanding Material Topics

What are Material Topics?

Topics that represent your organization's most significant impacts on the economy, environment, and people, including human rights.

Step-by-Step Process:
1. Understand Context

Map your activities, business relationships, and stakeholders

2. Identify Impacts

Identify actual and potential positive and negative impacts

3. Prioritize

Assess significance and prioritize most important impacts

Due Diligence Process

Due Diligence is the process to identify, prevent, mitigate, and account for how you address actual and potential negative impacts.
Key Steps
  1. Embed responsible business conduct into policies and management systems
  2. Identify and assess actual and potential adverse impacts
  3. Cease, prevent, or mitigate adverse impacts
  4. Track implementation and results
  5. Communicate how impacts are addressed
  6. Provide or cooperate in remediation when appropriate
Implementation Tips
  • Start with high-risk areas and activities
  • Involve relevant stakeholders in the process
  • Use existing risk management systems where possible
  • Document decisions and actions taken
  • Review and update regularly

Best Practices & Tips

Do's
  • Start early and plan your reporting timeline
  • Engage stakeholders throughout the process
  • Use data management systems for consistency
  • Seek external assurance for credibility
  • Make reports accessible and user-friendly
Don'ts
  • Don't cherry-pick only positive information
  • Don't ignore stakeholder concerns
  • Don't report without proper data verification
  • Don't use technical jargon without explanation
  • Don't delay addressing identified issues
Remember: GRI reporting is not just about compliance—it's about driving meaningful change and demonstrating your commitment to sustainable development.
